The problem

Most outreach systems are built wrong.

They're built for sales teams with ops budgets. Pipelines, deal stages, dashboards, automation — all of it needs setting up, maintaining, and learning.

You open them and feel the weight of it before you've sent a single email. Most people doing outreach don't need any of that. They just need something that keeps them on track.

Outreach works.
Consistency breaks.

Most outreach doesn't fail because the message is bad.

It fails because follow-ups stop happening. A quick check-in. A friendly nudge. Those small actions create conversations. And conversations create opportunity. But only if they actually happen.

The real problem

It's not sending emails. It's remembering the next one.

Not a CRM. Not a cold-email blaster. Just a simple tool that keeps your outreach moving.
